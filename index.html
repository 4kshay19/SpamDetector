How the sorter thinks

  1. Strip it down

    Text is lowercased, links and numbers are removed, punctuation is stripped, so the machine judges words, not formatting.

  2. Weigh the words

    Each remaining word (and word pair) is scored with TF-IDF — common filler words count for little, distinctive words count for more.

  3. Compare to what it's learned

    A Naive Bayes model, trained on labeled spam and ham messages, compares those weighted words against the patterns it saw during training.

  4. Stamp a verdict

    Whichever label — SPAM or NOT SPAM — is more probable given the words in the message gets stamped, along with a confidence score.
